What Happened to Julian

A factual timeline of two preventable medical failures — documented by evidence and the treating physician's own admission

Photo: Julian on a bike ride, full of life
The Snakebite

Julian suffers a snakebite and is rushed to the hospital

While on a bike ride, Julian was bitten by a venomous snake. He went to the hospital expecting to be saved. He was conscious, alert, and alive when he got there.

Photo: Hospital exterior or treatment area
Failure #1: Under-Dosing

Julian is given less than the standard of care to save his life

FDA protocols require a specific number of anti-venom vials to save a life. Julian's hospital gave him less than the approved standard. His own doctor later admitted, "I tried to get him more."

"I tried to get him more."

— The treating physician's own words

Photo: Julian's father or family at hospital
The Broken Promise

A father begs for his son to be protected. They promise, but fail to act.

Julian's father asked the nursing staff to restrain his son to protect him while he was vulnerable. They promised him they would. They did not.

A father did everything he could to protect his son. The hospital made a promise to that father's face and broke it.

Photo: Hospital room or medical equipment
Failure #2: No Restraints

Left unrestrained, Julian suffers a fatal complication

Left without the restraints his father requested, Julian pulled out his own tracheostomy tube while being weaned off sedation. Standard medical practice requires physical restraints during this exact window. They were not there.

Photo: Memorial image or Julian with loved ones
The Result

We lose Julian to preventable hospital failures

Julian didn't die from the snakebite. We lost him to preventable hospital failures. He was under-dosed on medication and left unprotected after his family was promised he would be safe.

Why We Are Fighting

What happened to Julian should never happen to another family.

This hospital is still operating. These protocols haven't changed. We are fighting for answers for Julian, but also for the next son, daughter, brother, or friend whose family will trust a hospital to do what it promises.

Your support helps ensure no one else has to bury someone they love over a preventable failure.
